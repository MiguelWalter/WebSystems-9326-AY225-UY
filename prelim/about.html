About Philippine Red Cross

Our mission is to alleviate human suffering, protect life and health, and uphold human dignity, especially during emergencies.

Our Mission

Our mission is to provide quality humanitarian services to those in need, focusing on health, disaster response, and community-based programs that help build resilience.

Our Vision

We aim to be the leading humanitarian organization in the Philippines, known for saving lives, alleviating human suffering, and promoting peace and dignity in the face of adversity.

Core Values

  • ✓ Humanity: We are committed to helping those in need and alleviating human suffering.
  • ✓ Impartiality: We are neutral and do not discriminate based on nationality, race, religion, or political opinions.
  • ✓ Independence: We operate autonomously and are governed by humanitarian principles.
  • ✓ Voluntary Service: Our work is supported by volunteers giving their time and skills.

History of the Philippine Red Cross

1899, February 17

Through the initiative of Apolinario Mabini, the Malolos Republic approved the Constitution of the National Association of the Red Cross. Mrs. Hilaria del Rosario de Aguinaldo was appointed President of the Association.

1900, August 29

Felipe Agoncillo met with Gustave Moynier, an original member of the Committee of Five and ICRC President, for the recognition of the Filipino Red Cross Society and application of the 1864 Geneva Convention during the Filipino-American war.

1905, August 30

The Philippine Branch of the American National Red Cross (ANRC) was organized by Filipino and American leaders at the Ayuntamiento.

1917, December 4

The Philippine Branch was officially recognized as a Chapter of the ANRC.

1934

President Manuel L. Quezon took the initiative to establish an independent Philippine Red Cross. Not until the Philippines became independent did this materialize because the Commonwealth cannot sign the Geneva Conventions.

1942

A Japanese-controlled Philippine Red Cross was created to take care of internment camps located in the country.

1945

Upon the liberation of Manila, local Red Cross officials and the ANRC undertook to reconstitute the organization.

1946, December 1

Thirty-six Red Cross chapters were initially set up in the country. The first Filipino Red Cross Manager, Dr. J. Horacio Yanzon, was appointed.

1947, February 14

President Manuel A. Roxas signed the Treaty of Geneva and the Prisoners of War Convention.

1947, March 22

President Roxas signed the Philippine Red Cross (PRC) Charter, Republic Act 95.

1947, March 29

Mrs. Aurora Aragon Quezon, the first PRC Chairman, received a cable from Geneva indicating that the ICRC approved to recognize the PRC.

April 15

A humanitarian organization that serves as an arm of help during disasters and emergencies was established. The Philippine Red Cross (PRC) was inaugurated in colorful ceremonies at the Malaca√±ang Palace.

Sept. 17

The PRC was admitted as a bona fide member of the League of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies (now known as the Federation).

1981, July 31

Blessing and inauguration of the Philippine National Red Cross Headquarters at Bonifacio Drive, Port Area, Manila.

1992

By 1992, PNRC had already made its mark not only in Blood Services but also in Disaster Responses.

2003, September 2

The new PNRC hotline, #527-0000, was established to immediately respond to emergencies.

2004

January 15: Richard Gordon was elected by his peers as Chairman of the PNRC.

2006

February 4: Chairman Gordon was first on the ground to instill order in the chaotic rescue effort and recovery of bodies at the ULTRA Stampede during the Wowowee anniversary show.

2007

The PNRC Operations Center (OpCen) launched its Power Text Messaging System, a nationwide texting system for emergency alerts.

2013

The PRC leadership called for an emergency meeting with Movement Partners to address the needs of vulnerable populations affected by Typhoon Yolanda (Haiyan).
